SensorlessVLA: A Sensorless Joint VLA-Control Contact-Rich Robotic Manipulation Framework

Contact-rich manipulation without additional external force/torque or tactile sensors.

The paper studies whether visual-language-action policies can handle contact-rich manipulation using onboard interaction traces instead of calibrated force/torque or tactile hardware. SensorlessVLA conditions action generation on residual histories derived from motor-current effort and predicts motion, gripper, and stiffness commands together.

No external F/T or tactile sensors Real-robot contact tasks Motion + gripper + stiffness

Problem Formulation

Problem And Approach

Contact-rich tasks need both task-level grounding and compliant physical interaction, but common VLA interfaces usually expose only kinematic action channels.

Motion-Only Gap

These motion-only chunks can describe where the robot should move, but they do not explicitly include compliance or stiffness commands. This makes approach, first contact, and sustained contact hard to handle with a single learned motion stream.

Sensorless Context

Instead of adding explicit force/torque, tactile, or wrench observations, the policy receives onboard residual history derived from motor-current effort after nominal compensation. The residual history is treated as an imperfect temporal trace of physical interaction.

Hybrid Action

The policy generates a hybrid action chunk containing joint-space motion references, gripper commands, and log-coordinate Cartesian translational stiffness. Stiffness is therefore learned jointly with motion, rather than fixed as a downstream gain.

Method

Interaction-Conditioned VLA-Control

A slow VLA predicts a hybrid action chunk; a fast impedance controller executes the commanded stiffness during contact.

At each decision step, external and wrist images, language, proprioception, noisy action tokens, flow time, and residual history feed the slow interaction-conditioned flow VLA. The Sensorless Interaction Encoder maps the residual-history window into interaction tokens, and the Sensorless Interaction Adapter injects those tokens into late Action-Expert Blocks through gated cross-attention before the flow readout predicts the hybrid chunk.

Sensorless Interaction Adapter

Residual-history tokens condition late action-expert blocks through gated cross-attention.

Fast Impedance Execution

Predicted log-stiffness is converted into controller-rate Cartesian translational stiffness.

Bilateral Teleoperation Supervision

Demonstrations provide controller-derived compliance-intent labels without external F/T or tactile sensing.

Evaluation Protocol

Task Suite

Experiments use four real-robot contact tasks with matched data, observations, demonstration budget, randomization, and rollout ordering across methods.

Each method is evaluated with 30 rollouts per task. The VLA is queried at 10 Hz, while the controller-side command adapter and fast impedance controller execute at the hardware-compatible control rate. Success requires task completion and contact quality, so the evaluation counts both whether the target is completed and whether contact is maintained appropriately.

Whiteboard Erasing

Sustained planar contact while removing marker traces from a target region.

Whiteboard Writing

Fine marker contact while drawing a continuous S-shaped trace without losing or overloading contact.

Ethernet Cable Insertion

Contact-guided alignment and seating under tight connector tolerance.

Curved-Surface Wiping

Maintaining tool contact while the local surface normal changes along the stroke.

SensorlessVLA reaches 72.5% average success, compared with 32.5% for the strongest same-family motion-only baseline and 55.0% for the same-architecture fixed-stiffness baseline. The manuscript interprets this gap as evidence that residual-conditioned, time-varying stiffness helps across both sustained contact and phase changes, while the plotted stiffness traces show commanded impedance parameters rather than measured forces.
